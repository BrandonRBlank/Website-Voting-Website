Flying Private Jets

Like all natural processes, flying private jets increases entropy in the universe because it is an inefficient process. For example, upon running into flocks of birds, the jet engines may become clogged, thus reducing overall performance of the aircraft for a given energy input. Any energy not used for moving the aircraft is converted to heat, which quickly diffuses across the planet's surface. In addition, jet engines produce a myriad of poisonous chemicals, many of which act as the glass in a greenhouse and prevent heat from escaping into space.

Breathe Lightly

This is perhaps the most effective way to reduce carbon dioxide and increase oxygen levels in the atmosphere. The logic is quite simple: when you breathe, you inhale oxygen. During cellular respiration, the oxygen you breathe is absorbed into your bloodstream, thus reducing the oxygen levels in the atmosphere. At the same time, carbon dioxide and water are exhaled, thus increasing the atmosphere's level of carbon dioxide. Breathing lightly enables you to reduce your consumption of oxygen and expulsion of carbon dioxide. While this may be may be uncomfortable or difficult to do at first, there are a number of factors that will make this easier over time:

  • Weakening of the nerves that control breathing. When this happens, you will feel less of an urge to breathe.
  • Changes in blood chemistry. Higher levels of carbon dioxide increase the acidity of your bloodstream. However, over time, the carbonic acid in your blood will fall out of solution, thus increasing the pH to normal levels, thus reducing discomfort.
  • Evolution. Over many years, the human race will evolve ways of coping with lower blood oxygen levels and higher CO2 levels.

Breathing Note Low blood oxygen levels can lead to hypoxia, which may be fatal if prolonged. Be sure to have another individual present when performing this task.
